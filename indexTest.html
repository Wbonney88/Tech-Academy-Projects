About Me

My recent journey started around May of 2016. At the time I was working as an accountant for a property management company. I had always been mostly unhappy working as an accountant. I got into it thinking I would be analyzing financial data and solving complex math puzzles, but in reality for most accountants it’s mostly busy work with little to no opportunities to be creative. In fact most “creative” accountants eventually wind up in prison. Then everything changed. I had some medical issues that while not normally serious were exacerbated by one complication after the other. When all was said and done I found I had slightly high blood pressure and cholesterol. That coupled with a family history of heart problems really made me take a step back and examine my life. One test my cardiologist did was to have me hooked up for 48hrs to a Holter monitor to monitor my heart rate throughout the day. As we went over the results one thing became clear, accounting was literally bad for my health. As I examined my life one question kept popping in my head. If accounting doesn’t make me happy, and it’s bad for my health then why am I doing it? As I thought more and more about it I knew I needed to make a change. One of the things I liked the most about my job in accounting was the software side of things. Specifically querying databases to create custom reports, and resolving issues with the ERP system or other application. Because of that I decided to take my career in a more tech related direction, and I started taking computer classes at a local community college. I knew right off the bat that I was headed in the right direction. I fell in love with programming. It offers me ample opportunities to be creative while still challenging me with complex puzzles. I made up my mind and I just committed. I quit my accounting job, so that I could focus on learning programming through The Tech Academy in Portland. I’m now currently seeking work as a developer. I also see the value of having someone with an accounting background who also knows programming to work in an IT/Business Systems type role, and I’m very open to discussing that as well.
